Lambda

Lambda is "run this when X happens."

  • ·  You don't manage servers; AWS runs your code.
  • ·  Pay for invocations + duration + memory.
  • ·  Short-lived by design (max execution per invocation).

Lambda's killer use case isn't replacing EC2 — it's gluing AWS services together: APIs, scheduled jobs, file-upload triggers, queue consumers.
