Point-in-time copies

Backups are point-in-time copies you can rewind to.

  • ·  Databases — RDS automated + manual snapshots; native tools for DBs on EC2.
  • ·  Disks — EBS snapshots (incremental, Regional, stored in S3 behind the scenes).
  • ·  Objects — S3 versioning + replication + lifecycle.
  • ·  Centralised — AWS Backup schedules across resources and accounts.
habits Back up to your RPO. Retain for compliance. Separate copies (AZ / Region / account). Test restores. Encrypt.
A backup you've never restored is a promise, not a proof.
"Test restores" is the point to drive home. Most outages-during-recovery happen because someone discovers the backup is unrestorable on the worst day.
